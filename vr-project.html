Project:

VR Game

A project to answer the following question:

How can we make a VR game to showcase the possibilities of the AI, 3D modeling, and game societies?

Skills / Tools Used

Blender
Unity
Collaboration
Concept Art

Blender was used to create the 3D models for the game, all of which were original. While my primary role was management, I also created one of the assets.

Unity was used as the game engine, on top of which the game was built.

The project was a collaboration between the UCL AI Society, of which I was the head of Marketing; the 3D modeling society and the games society. This collaboration was initiated and organized by myself.

I designed all the concept art.

My Role

Creative director, Product manager, Project manager.

My Contribution

I utilized my position as the new head of marketing at the AI society to reach out and make connections with 3d modeling and game societies to bring together a team of 14 to create a VR game.


What Was the Challenge?

Creating a game that would effectively showcase the talents of the developers, while being achievable within a year using a team of developers new to game development, and especially VR game development.

Final Product

A fully playable VR game for the Quest 2, with AI, 3D modeling and sound design all included.

I made connections with the 3D modeling society and game society to collaborate in building a VR game. From the three societies, I created a management team of 4 to head the project, with myself as project manager, and three separate teams of between four and six people. Apart from a single member of the management team who was experienced in VR, the entire team working on the project had never developed a VR game, with most never having developed any game at all.


Once we organized teams, we had to decide on the vision for the final product. It needed to be something that we could finish within 1 year with our other workload and that would work within the Meta Quest 2 (which came with both technological and UX constraints).


To avoid issues of physical touch, we went for a shooting 'capture the flag' game. In order to make the most out of the VR, we went for a game set on the rooftops of the city. This also allowed for easy reuse of game assets in the environment. The AI aspect would be implemented through a pathfinding AI opponent and some AI that would find and shoot at you.


We experienced several setbacks throughout the project, including people having to drop out due to other engagements. Despite this, a core group of 5 of us maintained development throughout and we managed to finish the project within a year. While there are a lot of parts of the game that are far from perfect, this was an achievement for a team without any experience in creating a VR game (and most with very little development experience).

The final product was a cross platform (VR and web) game.
