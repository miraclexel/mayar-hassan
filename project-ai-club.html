Project:

AI Society Marketing

A project to answer the following question:

How can we rebrand to build community?

Skills / Tools Used

Graphic Design
Canva
Krita
Marketing Analytics
Behavioral Science
Social Media Marketing

I created all of the designs used for the branding (which could then be used as templates by members of my marketing team).

Canva was the primary tool used to create graphics.

Krita, an open source drawing software, was used to edit images for the graphic design.

Responses to campaigns in the forms of event attendance and social media engagement were analyzed to determine the effectiveness of campaigns and determine future campaigns.

Behavioral science was used to understand and identify areas of friction that prevented members from engaging with us.

I was in charge of social media marketing, including creating a strategy and executing it for YouTube, Instagram and Facebook.

My Role

Head of Marketing, Graphic Designer.

My Contribution

I pitched a vision for rebranding the society and creating a community; I brought on and led a group of 8 additional people to help execute my vision; and created and executed the vision.


What Was the Challenge?

To take a society that had a very low participation rate and completely transform it to have a strong brand identity and engaged member base.

Final Product

Graphics and projects collaborating with other societies. See the VR project as an example.

I joined the AI society as a funding manager, but noticed how the position that we were in as a society did not put us at any advantage when it came to getting funding, so I set out to transform the society by creating cohesive branding and identity. My transformational pitch can be found above.


The society had an issue with low member engagement and a lower profile compared to the competitor society (the data science society) which was larger and more prestigious. I always approach problems from a systems point of view, so I created a pitch that involved making the branding cohesive, building pathways for members to easily engage, and proposing links to other societies.



My proposal was accepted and I created the brand templates to use in social media marketing. These were then used by a team of 8 that I assembled to work under myself for the marketing.


My aim for the branding was to create a brand feel that was balanced futuristic with inviting, but also bold (the motto I created of 'Accessible, engaging, innovative'). As such the main colors were grays, navy blue and yellow, and vector graphics were often used to try to be more inviting compared to images.


Under my leadership, we also started to take a data-based approach to assessing the success of campaigns and events, A/B testing posts and tracking key performance indicators.


Another key part of my vision was a more engaged member base. One way to achieve this was the creation of collaborations with other societies to create projects, especially art-based societies.

One of these projects that I led was the collaboration with the 3D modeling society and the Game Society to create a VR game.
